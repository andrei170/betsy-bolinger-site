Executive Summary

One of the strongest agents in Central Texas — and one of the hardest to find online.

On paper, Betsy Bolinger is one of the strongest agents in Central Texas:

  • 20 years in business (since 2005).
  • Ownable specialties: VA / military financing, new construction, first-time buyers, relocation.
  • Fully bilingual (English + Spanish) in a market that rewards it.
  • Spotless "100% recommend" record across 17 reviews (per Facebook).

None of it lives anywhere she owns.

  • No owned site: betsyismyrealtor.com is unregistered.
  • Rented footprint only: a Facebook page + Instagram handle - platforms she does not control, neither of which ranks when a buyer Googles her name.
  • Result: a busy 20-year veteran is effectively invisible to every buyer who researches before calling.

The gap matters more than ever. Per NAR's 2024 buyer study: 43% of buyers start the search online, 100% use the internet at some point, yet 88% still buy through an agent. Buyers begin online and end with a Realtor.

  • Betsy owns the "end" - the referral, reputation, and record.
  • She is absent from the "beginning" - the search, the first impression, the capture.
  • Every buyer her reputation sends to Google today lands on a competitor or a directory profile that is not hers.

This is not a "you're failing" audit. It's a "you did the hard part and skipped the one asset that makes it compound" audit. She already has the trust most agents chase for a career. What is missing: an owned home base that turns 20 years of word-of-mouth into a findable, lead-capturing engine - and a defensible lead in her two best, most valuable Central Texas lanes: VA / military and new construction.

The fixes are surgical, not architectural, and they compound over 90 days.

Why this is Betsy's opportunity specifically

Her two headline specialties map onto the two strongest signals in this market:

  1. VA & military. Texas leads the nation in VA-loan volume; the Fort Cavazos corridor is one of the densest VA markets anywhere, with ~60% closing before they arrive. That buyer is remote, high-trust, expertise-driven - choosing on credibility and specialization. A PCS-ing buyer Googles "VA realtor Central Texas" or "military relocation Killeen agent" months out. Betsy does not appear today. She should own it.
  2. New construction. Austin is the #6 new-construction metro; new builds are ~38% of active listings and are cheaper than resale on median - a first stop for budget-conscious buyers, including military families. It's the lane where buyer's agents add the most value (the builder's rep works for the builder), where Betsy already has hundreds of closings, and one of the least-served search categories because most agents avoid it.

What the gap is worth (conservative, illustrative)

Directional estimates to frame scale, not promises:

  • Online buyers left on the table: a referral-based veteran captures almost none of the 43% who start online today.
  • Modest capture compounds fast: a handful of qualified VA / new-construction leads per month, at Central Texas ticket sizes, adds up quickly.
  • One extra closing per quarter attributable to the site (at the ~$411K new-build median, higher on resale) more than pays for the whole build-and-marketing engine for the year - before lifetime referral value.

The point is not the exact number: the reputation is already generating demand; there is simply no owned asset catching it.

The six vulnerabilities in plain language

1. No website at all. No owned asset - everything sits on rented platforms she can't rank or control. For a 20-year veteran, this is the single thing between "busy from word of mouth" and "the obvious first call in Central Texas."

2. Proof trapped on Facebook. The "100% recommend, 17 reviews" record is real and rare, but lives on one Facebook page - it feeds no Google presence and vanishes the moment a buyer searches her name on Google. Proof that can't be found doesn't convert.

3. No lead capture, no nurture. No site means no valuation tool, home-search, lead magnet, or email capture. Every visitor not ready to call today leaves with nothing and is never contacted again - pure leakage of reputation traffic.

4. Directory invisibility. No Zillow, realtor.com, or homes.com profile under her name. The platforms buyers trust are empty for her - while weaker agents named "Betsy" outrank her for her own name.

5. Unbranded Gmail. A generic Gmail instead of a domain address is a quiet credibility leak for a 20-year pro. The domain is available - a same-day fix once the site exists.

6. NAP / geography ambiguity. Austin branding + a (210) San Antonio area code + no confirmed office + no Google Business Profile means no consistent name-area-phone for buyers or search engines to anchor to. Consistency is a direct ranking and trust input.

03 — Competitor & Discoverability Landscape

The problem is not competition. It is absence.

Central Texas is one of the most competitive agent markets in the country: Austin alone has thousands of licensed Realtors, and the big portals (Zillow, realtor.com, Redfin) plus brokerage sites dominate page one for generic queries. Betsy won't out-muscle Zillow for "homes for sale Austin" - and doesn't need to. The winnable, high-value ground is narrower and far more defensible:

The name-search vulnerability

The most urgent problem is the simplest: when someone hears "call Betsy" and Googles her, they should find her.

  • Today they may find: a different Betsy, a stale directory entry, or a Facebook page - any of which can cost a warm, referred lead at peak intent.
  • The fix: an owned website + claimed Google Business Profile + directory profiles - solved in weeks, defended permanently.
  • This is the fastest, highest-ROI win in the entire plan.
04 — The 90-Day Plan

Surgical fixes that compound over 90 days.

Phase 1: Build the owned assetWeeks 1-2

  • Register betsyismyrealtor.com and deploy the website (this demo, finalized with Betsy's real headshot, real review quotes, and confirmed brokerage display).
  • Stand up branded email (betsy@betsyismyrealtor.com) and migrate off Gmail.
  • Wire Google Search Console + basic analytics.
  • Deploy schema: RealEstateAgent / Person (Betsy's credential stack), LocalBusiness, FAQPage, and Review markup so the 100% record is machine-readable.

Phase 2: Claim her name & reviewsWeeks 2-5

  • Create / claim Google Business Profile (once office / service-area is confirmed) - the fastest path to owning "Betsy Bolinger realtor."
  • Build out Zillow, realtor.com, and homes.com agent profiles under her name.
  • Migrate the reviews: systematic outreach to past clients (the 17 Facebook reviewers first) to re-post on Google, turning trapped proof into ranking proof. Target a steady cadence of new Google reviews.

Phase 3: Own the two lanesWeeks 3-8

  • VA / military landing page: PCS timelines, $0-down entitlement, appraisal rules, remote-buyer process, bilingual. Built to capture the corridor's high-intent, out-of-state buyer.
  • New-construction landing page: why you want your own agent at a builder, contract review, upgrade strategy, inspection timing.
  • First-time / bilingual page: English + Spanish, down-payment-assistance and Texas veteran programs.
  • Publish the lead-magnet guide ("Central Texas VA & Military Homebuyer's Guide") behind the email capture.

Phase 4: Local SEO & nurture flywheelWeeks 6+

  • Local SEO: consistent NAP across all profiles, service-area content for the corridor cities she actually works.
  • Review-request automation post-closing (keep the "100%" compounding, now on Google).
  • Simple email nurture for captured leads (guide download to consultation).
  • Monthly performance snapshot.
05 — Website Specification

Five tiers, one owned home base.

Tier 1 — Owned home base

Fast, mobile-first, single-brand site that ranks for her name and specialty. Navy + brass, Cormorant Garamond, "established / trusted veteran" feel. (Built - this demo.)

Tier 2 — Trust surfacing

"Since 2005" and "100% recommended" as hero signals. Credential bar (Since 2005 / 100% Recommended / Bilingual / VA-fluent). Real headshot + real Facebook review quotes on day one, with a plan to migrate reviews to Google.

Tier 3 — Specialty depth

Dedicated, schema-marked pages for the two ownable lanes (VA / military, new construction) plus first-time and relocation - each written for exact search intent, bilingual where it matters.

Tier 4 — Conversion architecture

Lead-magnet guide + name/email capture, click-to-call and click-to-email throughout, a "How it works" section, and an FAQ answering the VA / new-construction / bilingual questions buyers actually ask (all on-page today).

Tier 5 — Brand & compliance

Brokerage-neutral until confirmed. No invented stats, reviews, or facts. Any unverified item stays placeholder-flagged on the live build until Betsy confirms it.

08 — Risk Reversal

Owning her own name is the floor, not the ceiling.

  • Build tier: fixed scope, fixed fee, guaranteed delivery. Website, branded email, and core profiles ship regardless.
  • Growth tier - name-search gate: if she isn't on page one for "Betsy Bolinger realtor" within 90 days of the site going live and profiles being claimed, the next month of optimization is at no additional cost. Owning her name is the floor, not the ceiling - the outcome we're most confident committing to.
  • No long lock-in: the site is Betsy's owned asset from day one. If she ever walks away, she keeps the domain, site, email list, and reviews.

The whole point: she finally owns the thing her reputation feeds.

09 — Investment Framing

Start where it makes sense, scale as it proves itself.

Foundation

The "claim your name, stop the leak" tier: domain, site, branded email, Google Business Profile, Search Console, and one specialty (VA / military) landing page + guide.

Growth Recommended

Foundation + full two-lane build (VA + new construction + first-time / bilingual), directory profiles, review-migration campaign, lead-magnet funnel, local SEO, and the name-search performance gate.

Engine

The "compound it" tier: Growth + ongoing content, review automation, a paid-search layer for the VA / new-construction lanes, and monthly reporting - a durable lead machine.

Pricing is set on a fit call once scope and brokerage display are confirmed. The framing above is about sequence and outcome, not a quote.

10 — Why Now

Every month without the asset is warm traffic bouncing.

  1. The reputation already generates demand you can't see. 20 years of "call Betsy" sends people to Google today - and today they don't find her. Every month without the asset is warm, referred, high-intent traffic bouncing to a competitor.
  2. The two lanes are hot and open. Austin is the #6 new-construction metro; Texas leads the nation in VA loans; the Fort Cavazos corridor chooses agents on specialization. Exactly Betsy's specialties - and almost no individual agent owns the search for them.
  3. Owning her name gets harder the longer she waits. Other "Betsy" agents and the portals are consolidating the name-search real estate now. Claiming it in 2026 is a weeks-long project; reclaiming it after a competitor entrenches is a much longer fight.
